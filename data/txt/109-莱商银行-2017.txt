对授权授信、同业业务、票据业务等重点领域和关键环节开展风险隐患排查工作，并对检查过程、效果实施监督评价，堵塞违规操作漏洞。四是开展全员思想教育活动。通过开展“守纪律讲规矩”主题党日、行长大讲堂、廉政警示教育、预防职务犯罪专题教育等多层次、多维度的思想教育活动，明确了员工行为“禁令”和操作“红线”，引导员工牢固树立红线意识和底线思维。(四)流动性风险管理流动性风险是指银行虽然有清偿能力，但无法及时获得充足资金或无法以合理成本及时获得充足资金以应对资产增长或支付到期债务的风险。报告期内，公司从以下方面加强流动性风险管理：一是完善流动性风险管理体系。完善流动性风险管理政策流程，明确“两会一层”在流动性风险管理中的职责；建立流动性风险联动协作机制，形成流动性风险管控合力；建立完善流动性风险隔离和应急机制，防范流动性风险与其他风险的交叉传染。二是加强流动性风险指标监测报告。动态分析流动性比例、核心负债依存度、流动性缺口率等指标变动趋势，准确预测未来流动性需求，及时采取措施保障流动性充足；建立流动性风险日、周、月、年报监测报告机制，确保公司董事会、高级管理层及时了解流动性风险管理现状。三是定期开展压力测试。按季开展压力测试工作，综合考虑影响流动性风险传导的因素，采取动态流动性缺口模型实施压力测试，并根据测试结果适时修订流动性应急预案及调整资产负债管理策略。四是搭建流动性风险管理工具平台。年内建成上线资产负债管理系统，依托该系统逐步完善流动性风险管理与报告体系,提升系统在流动性风险管理中的应用水平。0（五）信息科技风险及对策信息科技风险是指信息科技在银行运用过程中，由于自然因素、人为因素、技术漏洞和管理缺陷产生的操作、法律和声誉等风险。报告期内，公司从以下方面加强信息科技风险管理：一是强化信息科技治理。明晰信息科技管理委员会的职能，厘清工作流程；修订信息科技制度44项，完善信息科技操作流程。二是完善信息科技风险管理工具。建立常态化的信息科技风险检测、预警与处置机制，充分应用信息科技风险管控系统，做好信息科技风险的持续跟踪监测。三是开展信息科技风险隐患排查。查摆风险管理流程及硬件设备方面隐患，并进行有效整改，持续完善信息系统基础建设。四是加强业务连续性应急能力建设。开展应急处置能力自查，建立重要领域风险排查清单；开展应急预案专项提升和统一备份专项梳理，整理应急预案58项，生产数据恢复验证49次；更新应急通讯录，与外包商、关联单位建立快速响应机制，提升业务演练的实用性。五是加强信息科技外包管理。严格落实信息科技外包安全管理措施，建立外包服务商评价和黑名单管理机制；开展外部服务商尽职调查，对外包活动的服务质量进行跟踪评价。六是强化信息安全管理。开展重要信息系统、互联网系统的安全评估，对电子银行、手机银行、门户网站等互联网应用系统进行渗透测试；开展各项网络安全自查和风险评估，实行内网准入控制，提升网络安全防控能力。七是加强客户信息保护。规范生产数据使用、提取和销毁等流程，提升客户信息安全性；部署外包虚拟化桌面环境，防止外包人员非授权获取数据信息；部署脱敏系统，有效防范敏感信息泄漏。八、内部控制制度的完整性、合理性与有效性公司持续以审慎经营、防范风险为出发点，本着全面、审慎、有效、独立的原则不断加强内部控制，全面贯彻执行各类内控制度，实现了各项业务的快速发展和稳健运行。报告期内，公司进一步完善制度生成机制，成立了制度评审委员会，对各部门、各条线的内控制度进行审议；印发了制度建设管理办法，对内控制度生命周期进行全过程管理，制度的完整性、合理性、有效性显著提升。同时，公司将2017年确定为“制度建设年”，结合组织架构调整，在广泛征询意见的基础上修订完善368项制度，基本形成了内容科学、流程严密、运行有效的内部规章制度体系。2017年，公司对内部控制制度的建设、执行落实情况进行了客观评价。根据评价结果，公司建立了能满足管理需要的内部控制体系，能结合发展需要不断改进和完善内部控制制度体系，相关制度覆盖了业务活动和内部管理的主要方面和关键环节，在规范财务会计行为、提高会计信息质量、强化经营管理、控制经营风险、堵塞漏洞、防止舞弊等方面得到了有效的执行。内部控制体系从内部控制环境、风险识别与评估、主要业务控制措施、信息交流与反馈、内部监督与纠正等方面，涵盖了内部控制的主要要素，内部控制体系基本完整合理，整体运行顺畅有效，基本达到内部控制的预期目标，内部控制体系不存在重大缺陷，符合监管要求和内部实际。Chapter Risks of the Company and risk managementThe comprehensive risk management system for the company’s risk management consists of 
the Board of Directors, the Board of Supervisors, the senior management, the comprehensive risk 
management department, the main control department for risk management, the responsible department 
for risk management, the internal audit department, and all branches. The Board of Directors is ultimately 
responsible for risk management. The senior management is responsible for formulating risk management 
policies and procedures and implementing overall risk management. The senior management has set up 
special committees such as the Credit Risk Review Committee and the Assets-Liabilities Management 
Committee, and also designated the main control departments and management departments of various 
risks. Sticking to the idea of comprehensive risk management of “all staff involved in risk control” , 
the Company has been constantly revising and perfecting the risk management system and operation 
procedures, and adopting the effective risk management and control measures. Major risks confronted by 
the Company during the reporting period include credit risk, market risk, operational risk, liquidity risk, 
IT risk, etc.(i) Credit risk management
Credit risk refers to the risks of economic losses to the creditors or financial product holders caused 
by the influence on the value of financial products resulted from failure to fulfil the obligations stipulated 
in the contract of or changes of the credit quality of debtors or counterparties. During the reporting period, 
the Company strengthened the credit risk management from the following aspects. Firstly, the guiding role 
of the credit extension policies was enhanced. The guidelines of the credit extension policies and main 
work points of the credit business line were defined, and various branches were guided to optimize and 
adjust their regional business policies, marketing policies, customer sand policies, and credit investment 
policies through measures of different classification and for different places so that the precision of credit 
investment was improved. At the same time, the policy tracking and evaluation were strengthened to 
ensure the real implementation of the credit extension policies and control requirements. Secondly, the 
Company perfected the credit risk management system. All in-balance-sheet off-balance-sheet businesses 
which undertake credit risks were included in the unified credit management; the standardization 
construction was carried out for the credit risk management system, and the credit risk early-warning 
and disposal mechanism was established and implemented; extended and cross review in which involved 
business lines participate was conducted for projects under credit extension; the construction of the review 
team was strengthened, the pertinence of the internal training was improved, and the professional talents 
of the review line were introduced actively; the construction of the disbursement center was promoted 
continuously, the implementation of credit extension and usage conditions was strengthened, and credit 
and loan was separated strictly; the normal mechanism of post-loan performance monitoring and check 
was established; testing and special inspection of credit risks were conducted in the rolling manner so 
as lock risk signs and place risk management pass ahead. Thirdly, the Company promoted the disposal 
of non-performing assets vigorously. The "334" strategy was fully implemented for the non-performing 
assets so that a plan was made for each of the non-performing loans and those non-performing loans were 
disposed properly by actively using lawsuits, asset transfer, risk agency and otherwise.
      (ii) Management of market riskMarket risk refers to the risks of losses of in-balance-sheet and off-balance-sheet businesses due to 
adverse changes in market price (interest rate, exchange rate, stock price and commodity price). During 
the reporting period, the Company strengthened the market risk management from the following aspects. 
Firstly, the Company perfected the market risk management system. The market risk management system 
was established to match with business nature, scale and complexity, make clear the responsibilities of 
"two boards and one level (the Board of Directors, the Board of Supervisors, and the management)", 
and main control and responsible departments of market risks, set up the clear market risk management 
policies and procedures, optimized the tools and methods to recognize, measure, monitor and evaluatemarket risks, and the explicit reporting route and way of information disclosure were defined. Secondly, 
the Company improved the authorization management mechanism. According to the risk characteristics 
of different businesses, the examination and approval authority of those have the right of trade and 
investment business and the operating authority of traders were defined clearly, and the internal control 
and supervision mechanism was established which is relatively separate in pre-, mid-, and post-authority 
to ensure effective supervision and operation of all transactions. Thirdly, the limit management was 
implemented. The limit system was set up, covering capital trading limit, investment trading limit, total 
position limit in trading accounts, stop-loss limit, and others; the limit management was implemented for 
bond investment trades, and different frequencies of revaluating market value were determined according 
to different trading purposes, so as to ensure all kinds of limits to get effective implementation. Fourthly, 
the Company optimized the asset allocation structure. The scale, category and duration of investment were 
controlled reasonably, and the outsourcing business scale was reduced moderately, so that the initiative of 
risk management was grasped, and the risks caused by market price fluctuations were avoided effectively. 
Fifthly, the Company strictly implemented the foreign exchange risk control by paying close attention 
to the situation of clients' settlement and sales of foreign exchange transactions in real time, carrying out  
flat trade in the foreign exchange market in a timely manner, and appropriately controlling the position of 
foreign exchange exposure and currency structure so as to effectively manage exchange rate risks.(iii) Management of operational risk
Operational risk refers to the risks of loss caused by imperfect or problematic internal control 
procedures, employees, IT systems, or external events. During the reporting period, the Company 
strengthened the operational risk management from the following aspects. Firstly, the Company 
established the tools and platform of operational risk management by starting the construction of 
the internal control compliance and operational risk management system, carrying out loss event 
collection, confirmation of key risk indicators, and self-assessment of operational risks and their control, 
implementing the capital estimation with the standard operational risk method, and further enhancing the 
fine management level of operational risks. Secondly, the Company seized the prevention and control 
of case risks by defining the key points of case prevention and control, clarifying the responsibility of 
case prevention and control, and actively looking for hidden risks; by conducting the investigation of 
employees’ behavior, with focus on whether employees participate in private lending, lending accounts, 
illegal fund-raising, and etc.; by printing and distributing the Management Measures for Home Visits 
to Employees, and timely understanding their thought fluctuations through strengthening the linkage 
between the unit and the family; by continuing to carrying out publicity, education and special inspection 
to prevent and deal with illegal fund-raising activities so as to prevent employees from participating in 
illegal fund-raising risks. Thirdly, the Company strengthened risk screening in key areas by developing

(iv) Management of liquidity riskLiquidity risk refers to the risks of inability to timely obtain sufficient funds or timely obtain 
sufficient funds at a reasonable cost to deal with asset growth or pay due debts although the bank has 
solvency. During the reporting period, the Company strengthened the liquidity risk management from the 
following aspects. Firstly, the Company improved the liquidity risk management system by perfecting 
the liquidity risk management policy process and clarifying the responsibilities of the "two boards and 
one level" in the liquidity risk management; by establishing the linkage and coordination mechanism 
for liquidity risks to form the combined force for liquidity risk control; by setting up the complete 
liquidity risk isolation and emergency mechanism to prevent cross-infection of liquidity risks and otherrisks. Secondly, the Company strengthened the monitoring and reporting of liquidity risk indicators 
by dynamically analyzing the change trends of liquidity ratio, core debt dependence, liquidity gap 
rate and other indicators, accurately forecasting future liquidity demand, and timely taking measures 
to ensure sufficient liquidity; by establishing the daily, weekly, monthly and yearly monitoring and 
reporting mechanism for liquidity risks to ensure that the Company's Board of Directors and senior 
management had a timely understanding of the current situation of liquidity risk management. Thirdly, 
the Company conducted regular stress tests by doing at a quarterly basis, comprehensively considering 
the factors influencing the conduction of liquidity risks, adopting the dynamic liquidity gap model to 
implement stress tests, and timely revising the liquidity contingency plan and adjusting the assets and 
liabilities management strategy according to the test results. Fourthly, the Company built a liquidity risk 
management tool platform. In 2017, the online asset & liability management system was established, 
based on which the Company gradually improved the management and reporting system of liquidity risks 
and improved the application level of the system in liquidity risk management.
(v)  IT risks and their countermeasuresInformation technology (IT) risk refers to the operational, legal and reputation risks of information 
technology due to natural factors, human factors, technical loopholes and management defects in 
the application process in banks. During the reporting period, the Company strengthened the IT risk 
management from the following aspects. Firstly, the Company enhanced the IT governance by clarifying 
the functions and working process of the IT Management Committee; by revising 44 IT systems and 
improving the IT operation process. Secondly, the Company improved the IT risk management tools 
by establishing the normalized mechanism for detection, early warning and disposal of IT risks, and 
making full use of the IT risk control system to ensure the continuous tracking and monitoring of IT risks. 
Thirdly, the Company screened hidden IT risks by checking hidden risks in risk management process and 
hardware equipment, carrying out effective rectification, and continuously improving the information 
system infrastructure. Fourthly, the Company strengthened the building of the business continuity 
emergency capacity by carrying out self-inspection of emergency response capacity and preparing a list 
of risks screened in key areas; by specially improving the emergency plans and unified backup plans 
through sorting 58 emergency plans and verifying 49 times of production data recovery; by updating the 
emergency address book, establishing the quick response mechanism with the outsourcers and affiliated 
units, and improving the practicability of the business practice. Fifthly, the Company strengthened 
the management of IT outsourcing by strictly implementing the security management measures for IT 
outsourcing, and establishing the evaluation on and blacklist management mechanism of outsourcing 
service providers; by carrying out due diligence of external service providers, and tracking and evaluating 
the service quality of outsourcing activities. Sixthly, the Company enhanced the information security 
management by carrying out security assessment on important information systems and Internet systems, 
and conducting penetration tests on Internet applications such as electronic banking, mobile banking 
and portal websites; by making self-inspection and risk assessment of network security, implementing 
the access control for the internal network, and improving the capability of cyber security prevention 
and control. Seventhly, the Company strengthened customer information protection by standardizing the 
usage, extraction and destruction procedures of production data to improve customer information security; 
by deploying the virtualization desktop environment of outsourcing to prevent outsourcing personnel from 
unauthorized access to data information, and the desensitization system to effectively prevent leakage of 
sensitive information.

照Chapter V Corporate Governance StructureI. Corporate governance of the CompanyDuring the reporting period, the Company has strictly observed the related provisions in 
the Company Law and the Law on Commercial Banks, conscientiously implemented the related 
regulations of the regulatory intuition on corporate governance, and been dedicated to further 
improving corporate governance structure, enhancing the effectiveness of the corporate governance 
mechanism and making the decision of the Board of Directors more scientific. The performance of 
the Company in its corporate governance is as follows:(i) Shareholders and shareholders' general meeting.The Company has convened the shareholders’ general meeting in accordance with the 
requirement of the Articles of Association and the Rules of Procedures for Shareholders’ Meeting 
and actively listened to shareholders’ opinion and suggestion to ensure that all shareholders have 
the right to know, to participate and to vote to the major events of the Company. During the 
reporting period, the Company held its 2016 annual shareholders’ general meeting, 2017 first 
interim shareholders’ general meeting, and 2017 second interim shareholders’ general meeting. The 
meetings went through related legal procedures in strict accordance with the related regulations to 
ensure that shareholders participate in the meetings and exercise their right. Also, legal opinion was 
issued by a lawyer.(ii) Directors and the Board of Directors.Currently, the Company has 11 directors, including 3 independent directors. The Board of 
Directors is able to conscientiously perform its duties in strict accordance with the provisions in the 
Articles of Association, make decisions with the authorization of the shareholders’ general meeting, 
strengthen the corporate governance, advance strategic steps and promote steady operation which 
safeguarded the interests of the Company and all shareholders. During the reporting period, the 
Company held 8 board meetings totally.Currently, under the Board of Directors of the Company is there 1 office and 5 special 
committees, including the Office of Board of Directors, Related Transaction Control Committee, 
Risk Management Committee, Strategic Development Committee, Audit Committee, and 
Nomination and Remuneration Assessment Committee. Under Risk Management Committee 
are the Special Committee of Reputation Risk Management and Special Committee of IT Risk 
Management. These 5 special committees have specific scopes of power, supervise and manage the 
matters at different aspects, and are accountable to the Board of Directors. They provide constructive 
suggestions for the decision making of the Board of Directors in a timely manner to improve the 
science of the decisions made by the Board of Directors.(iii) Supervisors and the Board of Supervisors.The Company has 8 supervisors, including 3 employee supervisors and 2 external supervisors. The 
Board of Supervisors conscientiously performs its duties in strict accordance with the provisions 
in the Articles of Association, carries out supervision and inspection over the compliance of 
the Company’s finance, risk management, asset quality and the duty performance of the senior 
management, communicates with the external audit units and management regularly or irregularly, 
conducts targeted special inspections, and puts forward suggestions and improvement opinions to 
the Board of Directors and the management according to hidden risks or problems discovered, so as 
to play the role of the Board of Supervisors more effectively.(iv) Information disclosure and transparency.The Company has published its 2016 Annual Report on Financial Times, and prepared a 
separate edition of 2016 Annual Report of Laishang Co., Ltd. (Summary), to carry out information 
disclosure in a timely and accurate manner and safeguard the interests of shareholders, particularly 
medium and small shareholders.II. Independent directors and their performance of dutiesDuring the reporting period, there were 3 independent directors on the Board of Directors. 
With an attitude of being responsible to all shareholders, these independent directors performed the 
obligation of integrity and diligence, earnestly participated in the board meetings and meetings of 
various special committees, and actively delivered their opinions to safeguard the collective interest 
of the Company and the legitimate rights and interest of medium and small shareholders.(i) Independent directors’ participation in meetings.During the reporting period, the number of independent directors attending the board meetings 
should be 21, with presence in 20 persons, entrusted attendance in 1 persons, and absence in 0 
person.(ii) Objections of the independent directors to related affairs of the Company.During the reporting period, the independent directors of the Company did not object to anyproposal of the Board of Directors of the current year.III. of Supervisors and all special committee meetings, positively gave opinions and prevent overall 
benefit of the Company and lawful rights and interests of medium and small shareholders from

and supervised through the Board of Directors and the Board of Supervisors. The president, 
employed by the Board of Directors, is responsible for all daily operation and management of 
the Company. Only the head office of the Company is its legal person, and all branches and sub-
